A portrait of East Harlem's "Little Mexico"

By: Dashiell Allen

10/16/2022

The community is concentrated on East 116th Street between 2nd and 3rd Avenues. A street sign on the corner of 116th and 2nd was renamed "Mexico-Tenochtitlan" last year.

Every year around Sept. 15, the community celebrates Mexican Independence Day with a festival full of food, traditional artisan goods, music and dance.

Concepción Sánchez, a former restaurant worker, opened "Artesanías Sánchez" two years ago. He creates piñatas and traditional statues using recycled materials.

Alejo Sánchez, 30, grew up in East Harlem to parents who immigrated from the States of Puebla and Guerrero. His father is the owner of Taco Mix, a late-night tacqueria that's been around for the past 25 years. Sánchez opened his own shop, Don Alejo Café, a few doors down from his dad on East 116th Street, last fall, bringing the new flavors of artisenal South American coffee to his community

New York City had a Mexican-born population of 155,628 people in 2019, making it the second largest Latinx foreign born group in the city.
