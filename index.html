A portrait of East Harlem's "Little Mexico"

By: Dashiell Allen

10/18/2022

The community is concentrated along East 116th Street between 2nd and 3rd Avenues. A street sign on the corner of 116th and 2nd was renamed "Mexico-Tenochtitlan" last year in a ceremony attended by Mexico's Foreign Minister Marcelo Ebrard.

Every year around Sept. 15, the community celebrates Mexican Independence Day with a festival full of food, traditional artisan goods, music and dance.

Concepción Sánchez, a former restaurant worker, opened "Artesanías Sánchez," a craft store, two years ago. He creates piñatas and traditional statues using recycled materials.

Alejo Sánchez, 30, grew up in East Harlem to parents who immigrated from the States of Puebla and Guerrero. Last year he opened his shop, Don Alejo Café, a few doors down from his dad's 25-year-old taqueria Taco Mix on East 116th Street.

New York City had a Mexican-born population of 155,628 people in 2019, making it the second largest Latinx foreign born group in the city.
